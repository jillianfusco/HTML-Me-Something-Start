From Teacher to Web Dev

My Career Journey

by Jillian Fusco

My Education Journery

I earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in Languages and Cultures from West Chester University in 2017, majoring in German with a minor in Spanish. As part of the immersion requirement, I had the privilege of studying abroad at ActiLingua Academy in Vienna, Austria, for four weeks. During this time, I completed C1-level courses, significantly enhancing my language proficiency and cultural understanding.
Upon graduation, one of my professors informed me about an opportunity to teach German at the West Chester Area School District. I was hired based on my bachelor's degree and granted an emergency teaching certificate by the state, allowing me to teach while pursuing my formal teaching certification. Over the next two years, I attended graduate-level classes after work to complete the requirements for my teaching certificate.

Traveling to Germany with Students

In 2022, I had the incredible opportunity to travel to Germany with my colleague and a group of 25 high school students. During our ten day trip we visited the cities Heidelberg, Ettal, Ulm, and Munich. My most memorable experiences were a bike ride along the Danube and a trip to the top of the Zugspitze, Germany's tallest mountain.

Getting my own classroom

My first 4 years of teaching I was a guest in other teacher's classrooms. After 4 years I finally got my own classroom to decorate and call my own!

Leaving Teaching

Throughout my tenure at the West Chester School District, I embraced the unique challenge of being a traveling teacher, moving between 2-3 school buildings each day. This role required juggling the varying schedules of each school, especially when middle and high schools had different start times. Despite these challenges, the experience allowed me to grow professionally and adapt to diverse educational environments and develop tools to become extremely organized and proactive. However, coupled with the myriad challenges of being a post-pandemic teacher, I ultimately decided to transition out of the profession in early 2023. Reflecting on these challenges, I came across an article that deeply resonated with my experiences:

People Don't Want to Be Teachers Anymore. Can You Blame Them?

by Jesse Grose
September 13, 2023

"The current state of the teaching profession is at or near its lowest levels in 50 years,” according to a working paper published in November by the Annenberg Institute for School Reform at Brown University.

Learning Programming

Reflecting on my teaching career, my favorite aspects were creating materials for my classes and mentoring students. I brainstormed ways to continue making things and teaching/mentoring and decided to become a web developer. I spent a few months doing self-paced courses like Scrimba and Zero to Mastery.

While both Srimba and ZTM were excellent introductions to web dev, I realized I needed something more structured and resource-rich.
After posting one of my projects on LinkedIn, a friend messaged me about an amazing free course from a nonprofit called LaunchCode, which happened to be accepting applications for their web development course. I was thrilled to be accepted and earn a spot, which I later learned had about a 20% acceptance rate.
I've been a LaunchCode student for almost three months now, and I can't believe how much I've learned in such a short amount of time. The class is very challenging, and the pace is intense, but I'm so glad I stuck with it because I've learned so much and feel confident that by the end of it, I'll be ready for an internship.
LaunchCode is split into three sections: learning JavaScript, learning Java, and what they call Liftoff, which includes career and interview preparation along with portfolio development. I'm nearing the end of the JavaScript section, currently working on HTML and CSS, and about to jump into learning React. It's been a great experience so far.
My favorite part of LaunchCode is the integrated community. I love having access to my classmates via Slack, and I have two amazing instructional assistants to answer questions and review my coding assignments with me. This supportive environment has been invaluable in my learning journey, providing both guidance and camaraderie.
